Current Work

Featured Research Projects

Melanocortin Receptor Signaling in Health and Disease

PI: Dr. James Wachira
Department: Biology

This project delineates the mechanisms of signaling through melanocortin receptors, which play important roles in diverse physiological processes including energy homeostasis, pigmentation, and inflammation. Understanding these pathways has implications for treating obesity, metabolic disorders, and inflammatory diseases.

Cell Signaling Molecular Biology Metabolic Disease

Developmental Neurobiology of Risky Behaviors

PI: Dr. Ingrid Tulloch
Department: Psychology

Using both human participants and animal models, this research investigates how early developmental experiences shape adult behavioral outcomes and cognitive aging. The work has important implications for understanding substance abuse vulnerability and developing prevention strategies.

Neuroscience Behavioral Research Drug Abuse

Community-Based Health Interventions in Urban Settings

PI: Dr. Payam Sheikhattari
Department: Public Health

This research employs community-based participatory approaches to address health disparities in urban populations, with a focus on tobacco control and nicotine dependence. The project develops and tests culturally-appropriate interventions that can be sustained in community settings.

Public Health CBPR Health Disparities

RNA Packaging and Gene Expression Control

PI: Dr. James Wachira
Department: Biology

Investigating the biophysical principles underlying RNA-protein interactions to gain insight into cellular RNA packaging pathways and the control of gene expression at the RNA level. This fundamental research has implications for understanding cancer, developmental disorders, and potential therapeutic targets.
